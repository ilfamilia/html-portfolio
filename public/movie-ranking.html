The Best Movies According to Me

My top 3 movies of all-time

Pulp Fiction

"Pulp Fiction" is a nonlinear crime drama film directed by Quentin Tarantino, released in 1994. The movie intertwines various interconnected stories in the criminal underworld of Los Angeles.

Fight Club

"Fight Club" follows the life of an insomniac and disenchanted narrator (Edward Norton), whose mundane job and empty consumer-driven existence have left him feeling disconnected from reality. Seeking solace in support groups for various ailments, he encounters another troubled soul, Marla Singer (Helena Bonham Carter), who shares his detached outlook on life.

narrator's life takes a drastic turn when he crosses paths with Tyler Durden (Brad Pitt), a charismatic and anarchic soap salesman. The two form an unlikely friendship and eventually establish an underground society known as "Fight Club." The club provides a cathartic release for men disillusioned by societal expectations and emasculating norms, offering them a place to physically and emotionally confront their pain.

Memento

"Memento" is a psychological thriller film directed by Christopher Nolan, released in 2000. The story follows Leonard Shelby, a man suffering from anterograde amnesia, a condition that prevents him from forming new memories. After his wife is brutally murdered, Leonard embarks on a quest for vengeance, relying on Polaroid photographs, tattoos, and notes to help him remember his purpose and the clues he gathers.
